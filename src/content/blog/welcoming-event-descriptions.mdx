_This article is targeted to event organizers, but attendees can help with this, too!_

## Lot of Questions to Answer!

When someone is considering coming to your event, they have a lot of questions! The more you can answer upfront, the more likely each person is to come.

Do a **self-check**: how many of these are covered in your last event description?

- Title
  - [ ] From the title alone, without context, would someone know what the event was? (I like: group name, event type, location)
- Group Ethos
  - [ ] What is the purpose/values of this group?
  - [ ] Who is this group for?
  - [ ] Who runs the group? (is it [community-led or company led](https://www.happyandeffective.com/blog/community-groups-community-led-vs-company-led)?)
  - [ ] Is it paid for by a company or sponsors etc?
- Event Details
  - [ ] What will we do there?
  - [ ] Does it cost money?
  - [ ] Covid details? (Ventilation, masks, etc)
- Getting There
  - [ ] Parking? Free/paid?
  - [ ] Public transit?
- Getting In
  - [ ] Once I'm there, how will I get into the building/room?
  - [ ] How will I know when I'm in the right place?
  - [ ] If I come late, how will I get in?
- Getting Involved
  - [ ] Can we talk with others somewhere outside of the event? (Slack/Discord?)
  - [ ] Can people like me get involved organizing/volunteering? How?

## Isn't that a lot?

It's longer than most descriptions, true. But it helps make your group inclusive! My big idea / goal here is to be inclusive of people who would love the event but hesitate to come -- due to fear of parking, uncertainty of what it is, etc.

You do want to make sure it's easy to skim! Headers, bold text, and most important info at the top. The reader can decide if they want to read it all or not -- some people definitely will, and some people definitely won't.
